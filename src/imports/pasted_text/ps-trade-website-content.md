**Intro**
From homes to farms to institutions, P.S. Trade and Suppliers engineers solar solutions built for Nepal's terrain, climate, and power needs.

**Solution Cards**

**On-Grid / Off-Grid Solar**
A solar panel is an electronic device made up of a collection of solar cells that absorb sunlight and convert it directly into usable electricity. We design and install both grid-tied systems for urban and commercial properties and standalone off-grid systems for remote locations without reliable access to the national grid.

**Solar Water Heater**
A solar water heater is a rooftop thermal system that absorbs heat from the sun and transfers it directly to water for household use — such as showering, washing dishes, or laundry — cutting electricity costs while reducing carbon footprint.

**Inverter Backup System**
An inverter backup system is an emergency power setup designed to keep your lights and appliances running seamlessly during a power outage or load shedding, giving homes and businesses dependable continuity when the grid goes down.

**Solar Water Pumping System**
A solar water pumping system is a highly efficient setup that uses electricity generated by solar panels to power a water pump — widely used for agricultural irrigation and rural drinking water schemes across Nepal, eliminating dependence on diesel or grid power.

## General SEO Recommendations

- **Image alt text:** Use descriptive, keyword-rich alt text for every photo (e.g., "78.84 kWp grid-tied solar PV installation at Siddhasthali Rural Community Hospital Hetauda" instead of "IMG_021.jpg").
- **Local SEO:** Set up and verify a Google Business Profile for "Sano Bharyang-2, Kathmandu" with the same NAP (Name, Address, Phone) used on the website footer.
- **Schema markup:** Add `LocalBusiness` and `Organization` structured data (JSON-LD) to the homepage, and `Person` schema on team member profiles if your site builder supports it.
- **Internal linking:** Link service and solution names in the Home page teasers directly to their full pages — this helps both users and search engines navigate the site.
- **Project pages:** Consider giving each "Recent Project" its own short page/case study (location, system size, client, outcome) — this is strong, low-competition long-tail SEO (e.g., "solar hybrid system Dolpa school").
- **Page URLs:** Use clean, keyword-friendly slugs, e.g. `/solar-services-nepal`, `/solar-solutions-nepal`, `/about-us`, `/our-team`.
